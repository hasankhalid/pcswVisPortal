Guiding questions

What is the incidence of psychological, physical and sexual violence by spouse in Punjab?

How are physical, sexual and psychological violence by spouse related?

Data collected in ESW 2018 survey reveals that different forms of spousal violence are related. The co-occurrence of physical, sexual and psychological violence by spouse can be studied by the Venn diagram below.

Each circle represents a form of violence and its area denotes the relative occurrence of that form of violence. The overlapping areas represent the relative co-occurrence between different violence forms. For example it can be seen that 5.3% of ever married women in Punjab experienced psychological, physical as well as sexual violence by spouse.

This significant overlap between different violence forms calls for an urgent need for policy interventions to spread awareness and discourage all forms of spousal violence.

Guiding questions

What are women’s attitudes towards 'violence against women' and how do they compare to men’s attitudes?

While designing interventions that combat all forms of violence against women, it is also important to discourage attitudes that support or reinforce violence against women. In ESW Survey 2018, data was collected on women’s (woman baseline survey) as well as men's (men's survey) attitudes towards physical and sexual violence by spouse.

For example, all women respondents aged 15-64 were asked if in their opinion, a husband had a good reason to hit their wives in certain specific situations. Women’s responses at a district level can be investigated through heat map below. The grid has district on the vertical axis and specific situations (coded by alphabets) on the horizontal axis. The percentage (who agree) is encoded by color depth. The visualization also shows the actual incidence of physical violence in the districts through bubbles on the right.
